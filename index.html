Before we begini

These letters are addressed to two strangers.

Neither of them has met the other yet. Both of them are about to. They just don't know it yet. And they wouldn't believe me if I told them.

So I'm writing ahead of them. To the people they were right before everything changed. To tell them what's coming, and to tell them, mostly, not to be afraid of it.

Turn the page slowly. There's no hurry. They waited a long time to find each other.

Dear Girl in the Beige Hoodie

You won't remember this, and that's all right. You weren't doing anything you'd think was worth remembering. You stepped into a lift at Mushkan Di's wedding in a beige hoodie, looking like you had somewhere to be, and the doors closed, and that was it. Four seconds. Maybe five.

Here's the strange part about those few seconds. A man you have never met is going to remember those few seconds far longer than he should. The hoodie. The way you stood. The fact that you had absolutely no idea anyone was looking.

You think nothing happened in that lift. I'm writing to tell you that everything did. It just hadn't been given a name yet.

Go on. The doors are about to open.

Dear Girl on the Stage

The next day, I saw you again.

And this time, you weren't trying to get somewhere. You already were.

You were up on the stage with a camera, right in the middle of all that wedding chaos. The noise, the relatives, the hundred small disasters every wedding quietly runs on.

And somehow, you were making it look like the most fun anyone in the room was having.

Everyone was watching the bride and groom. I understand why. That's who you're supposed to watch.

But I was watching the girl holding the whole function together and somehow making it look effortless. You had this energy, like the room belonged to you and you were just being generous letting the rest of us stand in it.

I didn't know your name yet, so I gave you one in my head.

It was supposed to be a joke.

It stopped being one surprisingly quickly.

Main Character. It stuck.

Dear Girl About to Receive a Message

It's almost one in the morning on the twenty-sixth of February, and somewhere a phone is about to light up. It's yours.

The message is from someone you don't know. He has rewritten it more times than he'll ever admit. Typed it, deleted it, walked away, come back. He has told himself you won't reply, partly so it'll hurt less when you don't.

It says that everyone remembers the bride and groom, but he remembers the girl who made the chaos look fun. And then, because he's run clean out of courage, it just says: Hi.

You're allowed to ignore it. You don't owe a stranger anything.

But I know how this goes. Don't leave him on read forever. He means it far more than the word can carry.

Dear Girl Who Does Not Know She Has Hazel Eyes

Right now, if someone asked, you'd tell them your eyes are black. You've believed it your whole life. You've wanted them to be hazel, quietly, the way people want small things they've decided they'll never get, and a long time ago you made peace with simply not having them.

I need you to hold on a little while.

Because there's a man on his way into your life who is going to notice something before you do.

Not because you're trying to show it.

Not because you're talking about it.

Just because one afternoon, the light is going to catch your eyes at exactly the right moment.

And he'll see what you've never been allowed to see.

They're hazel. They've always been hazel.

Keep wanting it for now. I promise the moment someone finally tells you is worth every year you spent thinking you'd been given less than you have.

You spent years wishing for something you already had.

Funny how that turns out to be true about more than your eyes.

A truth you've been carrying

Dear Girl About to Meet Me

For fifty-one days you're going to be a voice, a screen, and a name that lights up a phone at the worst, best hours of the night. There's a call coming, the sixth of April, 1:33 in the morning, that runs an hour and forty-five minutes, and somewhere in the middle of it he stops listening to what you're saying and starts listening to how you say it. He falls for your voice before he ever sees you in daylight. He'll deny the exact moment. He's lying.

Then comes the eighteenth of April. Quarter past noon. A mall, of all unremarkable places. He'll be in a black polo and navy jeans, pretending to be calm. You'll be in a white top with little black dots and light-blue jeans, and you'll look at him and say "Hiiii," with all those extra i's, and he'll say, "Hello, finally."

Fifty-one days, and the first real word he says to you is finally.

A voice is about to become a person.

Don't be nervous.

He's been waiting fifty-one days for this. Terrified is a very polite word for what's happening inside his head.

Dear Girl Outside Socialite

It's just past midnight on the nineteenth of April. You're standing outside a place called Socialite, about to do something that should be terrifying and somehow isn't.

He's going to hug you for the first time. He's going to kiss your forehead. And here's the part neither of you will be able to explain afterward: there's no nervousness in it. No awkward laugh, no fumbling, no is this okay. Just a quiet, ridiculous certainty, like two people remembering something rather than doing it for the first time.

You won't have a word for what just happened. He will, eventually. He'll call it home.♥

Not a place. You. The peace of you. The way the noise in his head goes quiet when you're near.

You're about to become home to someone, and you have no idea you're doing it. That's the most you thing about you.

Dear Boy Who Stopped Looking Forward to Birthdays

Truth is, you've cried on every birthday since moving to Kolkata.

Different reasons.

Same feeling.

You let it become an ordinary day to get through, because looking forward to it had only ever set you up to be quietly disappointed.

Easier not to hope.

I want to tell you something that's going to sound impossible from where you're sitting.

There's a person coming who will make you look forward to days again. Not just birthdays. Ordinary Tuesdays. Calls at one in the morning. The drive to an airport.

You've spent so long bracing for disappointment that you forgot the other thing days can do. She's going to remind you. Start hoping again, just a little. Practise.

Dear Boy Who Thinks He Is Hard to Love

This is the last thing I'll tell you, and it's the one you most need and least believe.

You think you're a lot. Too much in some places, not enough in others. You think loving you is a project, that anyone who takes it on will eventually get tired, the way you've quietly always expected them to.

So here's what I know now, from the far side of it: she doesn't find you hard to love.

She finds it easy.

Embarrassingly easy.

She loves watching you when you're doing absolutely nothing.

She'll say, "Bebu, camera pe aao."

And you'll say, "Coming."

Every single time.

And somehow, it never once feels like effort to either of you.

One day you'll realise she isn't looking for a more interesting version of you.

She likes this one.

The ordinary one.

The one eating, working, sitting quietly, doing absolutely nothing.

That's the secret you couldn't have known. The right person doesn't endure you. They just enjoy you being there.

You were never hard to love. You were just loving people who didn't know how. She knows how. You're almost there. Go and meet her.

Dear Stranger

I started these letters writing to two people I called strangers, a girl in a beige hoodie who didn't know what was coming, and a boy who was certain he already did.

I have to tell you something now.

The girl in the beige hoodie isn't a stranger anymore. She has a name I made up and she let me keep, and a quieter one underneath it, and a thousand small ordinary moments I'd choose over anyone else's grand ones.

And the boy isn't the same boy either. He looks forward to things now. He gets picked up from airports by a girl who smiles like she's happier to see him than he is to see her. He still remembers exactly how five white roses felt in his hands. He has a camera that exists for no reason except that someone he loves likes to watch him through it.

So there's really only one thing left to say, sent backward to before either of them knew.

If I could go back and tell either of those strangers one thing, I'd tell them not to worry. They're going to find each other. ♥
